    WEB CLIPPING SUMMARY

    The fungi in the soil have elaborate underground networks that they use to feed nutrients from one plant to another. Some of the networks are very large. A single subterranean network can carry nutrients over thousands of miles, from the depths of the ocean to the tops of the mountains. The fungi take in nutrients from one plant and, by means of the network, send them to another. The fungi are the world's original recyclers.

    Read in full

    Networks can intelligently reroute resources such that a community of many different plants operates as efficiently as possible. Myconetworks provide nutrients and water to plants in exchange for the sugars generated by photosynthesis. Networks also facilitate information processing and storage capabilities, for example, retaining immune-system like "memories" of previous threats to the forest. In another sense, the networks are intelligent in similar ways to how transportation systems form in such a way as to efficiently move freight and people with super-highways, hubs, and smaller "country roads" to less populated areas.

    Chapter 1

    Post harvest, Vic rides his bike to the grove to contemplate the mystery. With the corn plants gone and the fields flat, sparse and spreading monotonously into the horizon, he can hear the insect songs have synced in a way he has never heard before. The sound is all encompassing, an aural claustrophobia to replace the physical claustrophobia caused by the now harvested 10 ft tall corn plants. As he rides away he goes by his ecosystem tracker and realises it is making the same sound as the insects and with the same rhythm, like the conductor of an insect orchestra. Vic grabs the entire tracker and takes it to the house. He dismantles it to check what is happening and discovers a partially destroyed vibrating chip that makes the insect noise. His CA friends tell him it is an alpha feature they have been experimenting with that uses radio waves to attract a plant-positive insect community. Analysing the chip, he realises the accidental damage has made it much more effective; as he is looking at it in the microscope, several insects appear in his home. Vic knows that something is seriously wrong. As the number of freakish incidents multiply, he returns to his home lab in Illinois and tries to figure out what is happening. He destroys the devices in half his fields but the crops still flourish. He burns a field, the plants come back. He is lost and re-engages his expert community of friends who helped him the first time. Olida, the fungi expert, is one of his calls.
